**Crossmint ships the wallets. lobster.cash ships the CLI. I shipped the MCP bridge — and the skill the CLI is missing.**

## Why this title and not the others

I picked this over the other four candidates for four reasons.

**It treats Crossmint and lobster.cash as the incumbents they are.** Both "ships" verbs are past tense on purpose. Crossmint shipped the wallets. lobster.cash shipped the CLI. Neither needs me. The only thing I claim is the two gaps — the MCP bridge and the skill — and I claim them in first person.

**It is not a put-down of lobster.cash.** The sharper candidate title was *"The two things lobster.cash doesn't ship"* — it reads punchy but the tone tilts toward a takedown. lobster.cash is Fede's product. Fede is the skill certification gate. Picking a title that makes him defensive on paragraph one is bad strategy.

**It names the contribution without overclaiming.** "MCP bridge" is a bridge — infrastructure that connects two existing things. "The skill the CLI is missing" is narrow — one specific knowledge gap, not a replacement for the CLI. Neither phrase tries to sell me as the new center of anything.

**It is the title a reviewer remembers.** Adolfo is reading dozens of submissions. "Crossmint ships the wallets. lobster.cash ships the CLI. I shipped the MCP bridge" is a rhythm he can repeat to a teammate over Slack without checking the link.

---

## What the title commits us to

Every downstream artifact (blog post, thread, video script, campaign proposal) must be compatible with this framing.

- The MCP server is a **bridge**, not a replacement
- lobster.cash is **already working** — this is complementary, not corrective
- The skill fills a **specific technical gap** (Solana CPI inner instruction scanning), not a product gap
- Saul is a **builder extending existing infrastructure**, not a vendor pitching a platform
- Both artifacts are MIT, ready to fork into the Crossmint org the moment the team wants them
