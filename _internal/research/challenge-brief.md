## The Brief

You're joining Crossmint's DevRel team on Day 1. Your first task: pick one concept or product from our documentation that you think we're not explaining well enough, and make it better.

That's it. The scope, format, and angle are yours to define.

---

## What to deliver

### 1. A small demo or code artifact

Build something a developer can look at, run, or learn from. This can be:

- A GitHub repo with example code and a solid README
- A working app (even a basic one) demonstrating a concept
- An interactive tutorial, a Replit/CodeSandbox, a script — anything runnable

It should be small enough to complete in a day. We're not looking for production-grade, instead, we're looking for clarity and intentionality.

### 2. One piece of developer-facing content

Explain the concept or demo you built. Choose the format you think fits best:

- Blog post or tutorial
- Twitter/X thread
- Short video script
- Additional documentation
- Something else entirely

Pick the format based on the audience you're targeting.

### 3. A campaign proposal (1 pager)

Pretend you're presenting this to the team. Make the case for why this content is worth doing, and how you'd make it happen. Cover:

- **Why this campaign** — what's the gap, the opportunity, or the moment
- **What needs to happen** — content, distribution, who's involved (marketing, DevRel, any other stakeholders)
- **Expected outcomes** — what does success look like, and how would you measure it

Your coworkers are busy. Give them enough context to say yes quickly, not a document they need to schedule time to read.

---

## Ground rules

- **AI is expected.** Use whatever tools you want: Claude, GPT, Cursor, v0, anything. We're evaluating your judgment.
- **Pick something small.** Don't try to cover everything. One concept, explained clearly, is worth more than a broad overview of the whole platform.
- **Use what's real.** Base your choice on our documentation, our blog, or recent product news. Pretend you have full access to our Twitter, blog, and docs — publish wherever you'd actually publish it.

---

## What we're evaluating

| Area | What we're looking for |
|---|---|
| **Technical judgment** | Did you pick something meaningful? Is the demo accurate and useful? |
| **Communication** | Could a developer understand this faster than reading our docs? |
| **Audience instinct** | Does the format and distribution match where real developers hang out? |
| **AI fluency** | Did you use AI thoughtfully — or just dump a prompt and ship? |
| **Taste** | Does this feel like something you'd genuinely want to share? |

## A few hints

- Crossmint's core primitives are wallets, checkout (headless and embedded), and agentic use cases — any of these is fair game
- The best submissions have a clear opinion: *"this concept is confusing because X, so I approached it as Y"* — be critical, be specific
- A communication plan that names real channels, communities, and people beats a generic one every time — but keep it tight, your future coworkers are busy
